## Project Proposal

Quantum Butterfly Effect is a quantum-inspired decision-making game where players manage global development over a period of ten in-game years. The core idea is that every social decision creates multiple possible futures that exist simultaneously. Only when the player chooses to **observe** the outcome does one future become reality.

The experience aims to simulate the uncertainty and interconnectedness of global systems. Instead of presenting simple cause-and-effect results, the game models reality as probabilistic and dynamic. The player must think long-term, manage trade-offs, and understand that sustainable development requires balanced and responsible decision-making.

The goal is to create an educational but engaging simulation that combines quantum-inspired mechanics with social responsibility themes.

# Thematic Focus

The main theme of the game is **Sustainable Development and Global Responsibility**, aligned with the **UNESCO and United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)**.

The game specifically connects to:

- **SDG 4 — Quality Education**
- **SDG 13 — Climate Action**
- **SDG 3 — Good Health and Well-being**
- **SDG 16 — Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions**

The theme is not only conceptual but integrated directly into gameplay mechanics:

- Investment in education increases long-term stability and innovation.
- Neglecting climate policy increases environmental risk probabilities.
- Weak institutions increase instability and conflict likelihood.
- Health investment improves resilience against crisis events.

The game demonstrates how these areas are interconnected and how short-term decisions can create long-term consequences. The player experiences how difficult it is to balance competing priorities under limited resources.

The project supports **UNESCO’s mission of Education for Sustainable Development** by promoting systems thinking and awareness of global interdependence.

# Quantum Dimension

The game is **quantum-inspired at the mechanical and state-based level**.

## Implemented Quantum Concepts

### Superposition
Each player's decision generates multiple possible future states that exist simultaneously as probability distributions. These potential outcomes are visible to the player before final selection.

### Measurement (Wave Function Collapse)
When the player presses **“Observe Future”**, one possible future is selected based on weighted probabilities, simulating wave function collapse.

### Entanglement
Game variables are interconnected. For example:

- Education influences the economy and social stability
- Climate affects health
- Justice systems influence long-term peace

Changing one variable affects others in non-linear ways.

### Observer Effect
The act of observation itself determines which future becomes reality.

# Beyond Classical Probability

The design goes beyond a simple classical random system because:

- Multiple potential states are explicitly represented before collapse
- Interdependent variables dynamically shift probability amplitudes
- Outcomes are state-based rather than fixed linear branches
- The system simulates complex systemic feedback rather than isolated events

The game uses **quantum mechanics as a structural metaphor**, rather than only aesthetic inspiration.

# Game Mechanics and Rules

## Turn Structure
The game is **turn-based**, where each turn represents **one year**.

## Player Actions
Each year, the player distributes limited resource points among:

- Environment
- Education
- Health
- Economy
- Social Stability

The player must work within budget constraints.

## System Response

After allocation:

- The system generates multiple possible future scenarios
- Probabilities are calculated based on previous decisions
- The player observes the outcome (collapse phase)

## Constraints

- Limited resources per turn
- Random global events
- Interconnected variables (entanglement effects)

## Multiplayer

Initial version: **Single-player**

Future development may include **multiplayer interaction between different countries**.

# Winning and Losing Conditions

## Success
The player achieves a **sustainable and stable world after 10 years**, with balanced progress across SDG indicators.

## Failure
Failure occurs if:

- Environmental collapse threshold is reached
- Social instability exceeds critical levels
- Economic breakdown occurs

## Multiple Endings

The game includes several possible endings:

- Sustainable Future
- Technological but Unstable Future
- Ecological Collapse
- Authoritarian Stability
- Fragmented World

This reinforces the **probabilistic nature of the system**.
